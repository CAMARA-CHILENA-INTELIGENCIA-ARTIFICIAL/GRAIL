Methodology

How the benchmark was designed and executed

Corpus

Three Chilean legal documents on health rights for cancer patients:

Ley 19.966 (2004) — Health guarantee system (GES/AUGE)
Ley 20.850 (2015) — Ricarte Soto Law, high-cost financial protection
Decreto 54 (2015) — Implementing regulation

58 pages total, chunked into 35 text units at 1500 tokens each. GRAIL extracted 281 entities, 435 relationships, and 13 community reports.

Fair comparison

Both systems use identical infrastructure:

Same LLM: Qwen3.6-35B-A3B via DeepInfra
Same embeddings: Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B
Same chunks: Both read from partial_text_units.parquet
Same top-k: 10 candidates retrieved

The only difference: GRAIL adds entities, relationships, community summaries, and reranking on top of the raw chunks. RAG gets chunks only.

Scoring rubric

Each response scored on 5 dimensions (1-5 scale):

Correctness (35%) — Factual accuracy vs gold answer
Completeness (25%) — Coverage of all key points
Source grounding (15%) — Correct legal citations
Coherence (10%) — Clarity for a non-expert
No hallucination (15%) — Avoiding fabricated facts

Empty responses score 1.0 on all dimensions.

Question design

7 categories designed to test different retrieval capabilities:

Single Fact (5) — Needle-in-haystack baseline
Multi-Chunk (5) — Answer spans 2+ chunks
Cross-Source (5) — Answer spans 2+ documents
Procedural (4) — Step-by-step processes
Comparative (3) — Compare concepts across locations
Negation (3) — "Does X cover Y?" where answer is no
Global (5) — Bird's-eye corpus summaries
